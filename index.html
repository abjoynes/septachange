Change in Funding for Construction

To keep SEPTA running smoothly, there needs to be updates of the systems, fixing trolley cars and buses, and maintaining the stops and stations. SEPTA receives 60% of their funding from the state; however, the remaining 40% needs to be balanced by the federal and local government for SEPTA to allocate funds responsibly. Once these funds are received they must be allocated effectively, so all aspects of SEPTA can work better.

Change in Ableism

According to Merriam-Webster Dictionary ableism is, “discrimination or prejudice against individuals with disabilities.” In SEPTA there are many examples of ableism: only select stops have wheelchair accessibility, civilians do not move for wheelchair riders, and some services do not stop for wheelchair riders.

Security Protocols

Security in Philadelphia on public transportation is subpar in almost all aspects. Although there are security guards at some of the express stops, at most local stop there are not. There are usualy just SEPTA attendants and they can not stop all dangers.
